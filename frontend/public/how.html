How to design your garden — step-by-step

This page loads as its own site so you can follow a full walkthrough. Each step includes practical tips and expected outcomes.

1) Start: Choose an area

Click Start Designing in the app to begin. You will be asked to provide a rough area in square meters or feet. Be conservative — add a little buffer for pathways.

2) Draw Land Boundary

Use the land drawing tool to click and define the perimeter of your garden. The app will smooth corners — for precise shapes, zoom in and add extra points.

3) Light & Soil Considerations

Before placing plants, note the sun exposure and soil type. Full sun plants need 6+ hours per day. For shaded areas, pick shade-tolerant species.

4) Place Plants

Select plants from the library and click where you'd like to place them. Use the spacing hints to avoid crowding. For trees, place them first, then shrubs, then groundcovers.

5) Use Layers & Mood

Toggle lighting and preview modes to check how shadows fall at different times of day. The preview helps spot sight-lines and scale issues.

6) Save & Export

Use Save or Save As to keep different design iterations. Export produces a JSON file you can re-import later.

7) Practical Planting Tips

  • Group plants by water needs to simplify irrigation.
  • Use taller elements to screen views, low elements to define paths.
  • Leave maintenance space — avoid planting too close to walkways.

Deep Troubleshooting

If plants overlap or collide: select and nudge them, or use the spacing tool to automatically distribute within an area. When importing older designs, check that plant IDs resolve; you may need to replace missing species from the library.
